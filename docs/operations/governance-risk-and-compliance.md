# Governance, Risk, And Compliance

## Governance Framework

### Principles

- protect trust and evidence integrity
- avoid unsupported product claims
- preserve user privacy and access control
- document critical decisions and incidents

## Core Policies Required Before Launch

- terms of service
- privacy policy
- security policy summary
- data retention policy
- incident response policy
- acceptable use policy

## Compliance Requirements

### Immediate

- lawful handling of uploaded customer data
- secure authentication and authorization
- documented deletion and retention posture

### Near-Term

- enterprise security questionnaire readiness
- legal review of evidence and report language
- regional privacy impact assessments as needed

## Risk Register

### Product Risks

- false positives
- overconfident messaging
- confusing uncertainty handling

### Technical Risks

- dataset drift
- storage exposure
- queue failure
- insufficient monitoring

### Business Risks

- broad ICP dilution
- long sales cycles
- compute cost spikes

### Legal Risks

- overstating evidentiary admissibility
- misuse of reports
- privacy violations from retained uploads

## Audit Logging Requirements

- login and auth events
- upload events
- scan lifecycle changes
- result publication
- report generation
- admin actions
- data deletion events

## Threat Protection

- rate limiting
- upload validation and scanning
- secret rotation
- least-privilege service access
- WAF and abuse monitoring

## Data Protection

- encryption in transit
- private storage by default
- access control enforcement
- defined retention and deletion policies
- environment separation

## Review Cadence

- monthly risk review
- quarterly policy review
- incident postmortem for all material failures
