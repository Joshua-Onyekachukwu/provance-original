## 1. Positioning Philosophy

### The Core Claim

> *"Provance is not an AI detector. It is a **verification platform** that produces **defensible, evidence-first answers** — not black-box scores. We are the trust infrastructure layer for the age of synthetic media."*

### The Positioning Matrix

| Dimension | Competitors Say | Provance Says |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| **What you get** | "73% AI probability" | "Authenticity Confirmed — with evidence" |
| **How you use it** | "Paste URL → get score" | "Upload → multi-signal analysis → verdict → forensic report" |
| **What you leave with** | A number | A downloadable PDF with evidence appendix, methodology version, and chain-of-custody |
| **When it's uncertain** | "Real" / "Fake" (always) | "Inconclusive — here's why, and here's what to do next" |
| **Who it's for** | Developers (mostly) | Journalists, lawyers, trust & safety teams, developers |
| **The promise** | Fast detection | Defensible verification |

### The Category We Are Creating: "Trust Infrastructure"

We are not entering the "AI detection" category — we are **creating** the "trust infrastructure for synthetic media" category. This is the key GTM insight.

**Category definition:** *Trust Infrastructure* is the systematic, transparent, and auditable process of verifying whether media is authentic or synthetic — producing an artifact that can be cited, shared, and relied upon in high-stakes environments.

**The categories we reject:**
- ❌ "AI Detection" — connotes black-box, score-only, probabilistic guessing
- ❌ "Deepfake Scanner" — limited scope, implies one-dimensional analysis
- ❌ "Content Moderation" — too broad, associated with censorship

**The category we claim:**
- ✅ "Trust Infrastructure" — systematic, defensible, enterprise-grade
- ✅ "Media Verification" — precise, professional, evidence-based
- ✅ "Forensic Analysis" — courtroom-ready, methodology-driven

## 2. Category Creation Strategy

### 2.1 The "Why Now" Narrative

Three forces converging in 2026:

1. **Regulatory mandate** — EU AI Act requires synthetic media labeling; US states (CA, NY, TX) passing deepfake disclosure laws. Legal teams need provable, admissible methods.
2. **Professional demand** — Newsrooms burned by fake UGC; law firms seeing deepfake evidence in discovery; security teams fighting AI-generated fraud. The market *knows* detectors don't work — they're ready for a better answer.
3. **Technical maturity** — Multi-signal forensic analysis, C2PA standards, and efficient model architectures have reached production reliability.

### 2.2 The "Better Mousetrap" One-Liners

For different audiences:

| Audience | One-Liner |
|----------|-----------|
| **Journalist** | "The first verification platform that gives you a report you can cite — not a score you can't explain." |
| **Lawyer** | "Forensic-grade evidence for synthetic media, designed for admissibility." |
| **Trust & Safety Lead** | "From 'I think this is fake' to 'here's the evidence hierarchy.'" |
| **Developer** | "A verification API that returns explainable signals, not a single float." |
| **Investor** | "We are the trust infrastructure layer for the internet's biggest unsolved problem." |

### 2.3 The "Never Say" List

These words reinforce the old category. We avoid them in all public-facing copy:

| Avoid | Replace With |
|-------|-------------|
| "AI Detector" | "Media Verification Platform" |
| "Detection Score" | "Verdict" or "Forensic Classification" |
| "Fake" | "Synthetic" or "AI-Generated" |
| "Real" | "Authentic" |
| "Accuracy" | "Forensic Confidence" |
| "Probability" | "Evidence Strength" |
| "False Positive" | "Inconclusive" (redirect to our honest uncertainty) |

### 6.2 Flywheel-Enabling Features (Product Must-Haves)

| Feature | Marketing Impact | Priority |
|---------|-----------------|----------|
| "Verified by Provance" watermark on every PDF | Drives organic discovery from report citations | **P0** |
| QR code linking to methodology page | Creates traceable attribution path | **P0** |
| Citation export (APA/MLA/Chicago/Bluebook) | Makes reports citable in articles, briefs | **P0** |
| Share link (time-limited, revocable) | Enables stakeholder review without download | **P1** |
| Embedded methodology URL in watermark | Provides transparency → builds trust → drives conversion | **P1** |
| "API Verification by Provance" badge | Enterprise integrations drive platform visibility | **P2** |

### 6.3 The "Provance Citation Index"

**Concept:** Track every public citation of a Provance report (via unique report URLs) and aggregate them into a public "Provance Citation Index" — a living map of where Provance reports are being used in news articles, court filings, and compliance documents.

**Marketing value:**
- Social proof at scale ("Provance was cited in X major investigations this month")
- Authority building in the trust infrastructure category
- Network effects — each citation begets more awareness

## 9. Risk & Contingency

### 9.1 Risk Assessment

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Waitlist growth too slow** | Medium | High | Double-down on Sample Report as lead magnet; launch "Forensic Benchmark" early; increase LinkedIn/Twitter cadence |
| **Design partner pipeline stalls** | Medium | High | Expand target list to 50 orgs; offer free trial before product is ready (guarantee early access); use team networks aggressively |
| **Email engagement below target** | Medium | Medium | A/B test subject lines; segment nurture by role; test deliverability; reduce frequency if needed |
| **Competitors claim "trust infrastructure"** | Low | Medium | First-mover advantage in category definition; double-down on "forensic report artifact" differentiator |
| **Product delay pushes beta back** | Medium | High | Keep waitlist warm with exclusive content series; offer "beta preview" to top 10 design partners regardless of product readiness |
| **Wrong positioning (too complex for early adopters)** | Medium | Medium | A/B test landing page messaging; survey waitlist at signup; iterate quickly based on email response |
| **"Inconclusive" verdicts frustrate trial users** | Medium | Low | Educate upfront (methodology page, welcome email); position uncertainty as a feature, not a bug |

### 9.2 Contingency Plays

**If waitlist < 50 signups by Week 3:**
- Run a targeted LinkedIn advertising campaign to journalists + legal professionals
- Offer "first 100 beta users get 6 months free" incentive
- Partner with journalism school verification programs (Columbia, Berkeley, USC)

**If design partner response < 5 by Week 4:**
- Hand-deliver Sample Report PDFs to target orgs (direct mail)
- Offer $500 Amazon gift card for completing 3 feedback calls
- Host a private "Synthetic Media Verification" workshop and invite targets

**If email CTR < 5% across all sends:**
- Pivot to plain-text, personal-looking emails from founder
- Reduce to 1 email per week with higher-value content
- Add "Reply to this email" prompts for dialogue
