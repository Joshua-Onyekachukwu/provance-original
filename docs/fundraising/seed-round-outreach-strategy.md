## 1. Outreach Philosophy

### The "Warm Introduction First" Rule
Investor outreach for an early-stage trust infrastructure company is won on credibility, not volume. Every introduction should be warm — through a mutual connection, an advisor, a design partner, or a cohort-mate. Cold emails to partners have <1% conversion at seed stage.

**Tier system:**
- **Tier 1 (Hot):** Warm intro, thesis-fit investor. Target: close within 4 weeks.
- **Tier 2 (Warm):** Introduced through network, needs education on category. Target: close within 8 weeks.
- **Tier 3 (Outbound):** Targeted cold outreach with research-backed personalization. Target: conversation, not close.

### The "Category Creator" Positioning
Every investor conversation must lead with the category, not the feature:
> *"We're not an AI detector. We're trust infrastructure for synthetic media — a new category that sits at the intersection of digital forensics, compliance software, and professional workflow tools."*

## 3. Outreach Sequence

### Phase 1: Pre-Outbound Preparation (Week 1–2)

| Action | Owner | Deliverable |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Create investor target list with contacts | Business Lead | This document |
| Prepare data room (organized index + linked docs) | Business Lead | Data Room Index (DATA_ROOM_INDEX.md) |
| Record 2-min Loom demo of product flow | Product/Design | Loom URL |
| Prepare 3-slide teaser deck (for warm intro forward) | Business Lead | 3-slide PDF |
| Secure 3 warm introductions from advisors | Founder/Lead | Intro emails |
| Publish "Provance Forensic Benchmark" blog post | CMO/Marketing | Public URL |

### Phase 2: First Outreach Wave (Week 3–6)

| Week | Actions |
|------|---------|
| **Week 3** | Send warm intros to Tier 1 investors (via mutual connections). Attach teaser deck + data room link. |
| **Week 4** | First meetings with Tier 1. Follow-up with data room + Loom demo. |
| **Week 5** | Tier 2 outreach (warm intros where possible). Follow-ups with Tier 1. |
| **Week 6** | Tier 3 selective outbound. Second meetings with Tier 1 prospects. |

### Phase 3: Diligence & Close (Week 7–12)

| Week | Actions |
|------|---------|
| **Week 7–8** | Due diligence calls — data room deep dive, technical deep dive, customer reference calls. |
| **Week 9–10** | Term sheet negotiation. Reference checks. Legal review. |
| **Week 11–12** | Close round. Wire funds. Announce. |

## 4. Meeting Agenda Structure

### First Meeting (45 min)

| Time | Topic | Who |
|------|-------|-----|
| 0–5 | Introductions & context | Founder + Partner |
| 5–15 | **Problem + Why Now** (The crisis of synthetic media trust) | Founder |
| 15–25 | **Solution + Demo** (Show the workflow: upload → verdict → report) | Founder |
| 25–35 | **Business Model + Traction** (4-tier pricing, Y1–Y3 projections, design partners) | Business Lead |
| 35–40 | **Competitive Positioning** (Why we win vs. Hive, Sensity, Reality Defender) | Founder |
| 40–45 | **Ask + Discussion** ($2M–$5M seed, use of funds, milestones) | Both |

### Second Meeting / Deep Dive (60 min)

| Time | Topic |
|------|-------|
| 0–15 | **Technical Deep Dive** — Multi-signal architecture, data loop moat, roadmap |
| 15–30 | **Financial Deep Dive** — Unit economics, growth model, burn analysis, scenario planning |
| 30–45 | **GTM Deep Dive** — Design partner pipeline, conversion strategy, enterprise sales motion |
| 45–60 | **Team + Hiring Plan** — Current team, seed hires, culture, governance |

## 5. Data Room Sharing Protocol

**Do:** Share the full data room after the first meeting. Use a password-protected Notion page or Google Drive with timestamped access.

**Don't:** Send the full data room cold. The teaser deck (3 slides) is the pre-meeting asset.

**Data room access levels:**
- **Stage 1 (Pre-meeting):** Teaser deck (3 slides) + Loom demo URL
- **Stage 2 (Post-first meeting):** Full data room link + cap table overview
- **Stage 3 (Due diligence):** Full access — all docs, financial model, customer references
