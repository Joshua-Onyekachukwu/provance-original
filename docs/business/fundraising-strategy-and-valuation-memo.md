# Fundraising Strategy And Valuation Memo

## Purpose

This memo sets a practical fundraising strategy for Provance based on:

- the current company stage
- the product complexity
- the AI funding environment
- realistic investor expectations in 2025-2026
- dilution discipline

This recommendation is intentionally more grounded than a hype-driven AI raise plan.

## Current Assessment

Provance has:

- a strong category thesis
- a differentiated product concept
- a good strategic wedge
- no live product yet
- no validated benchmark yet
- no pilots or recurring revenue yet

That means the company is **not yet in institutional seed shape**. It is in **proof-round / pre-seed shape**.

## Market Validation

Recent market signals support a disciplined strategy:

- Carta reported the median pre-money valuation on new U.S. primary seed rounds reached **$16M** in Q3 2025, showing that quality early-stage companies can still raise at strong prices in a selective market. [Carta Q3 2025](https://carta.com/data/state-of-private-markets-q3-2025-full-report/)
- AngelList reported **41.5%** of deals in H1 2025 went to AI/ML startups, confirming continued investor concentration around AI. [AngelList H1 2025](https://www.angellist.com/data-center/the-state-of-venture-1h25)
- Carta-linked and market summaries place AI seed valuations at roughly a **42% premium** to non-AI peers, with a cited median around **$17.9M** in 2024. [Seed Valuation 2026](https://hub.causo.ai/guides/seed-valuation-2026)
- Carta's Q3 2025 market data also shows investors remain selective even while valuations have improved, which means great companies can raise, but weak signal gets punished. [Carta Q3 2025](https://carta.com/data/state-of-private-markets-q3-2025-full-report/)
- PitchBook's Q1 2025 European VC valuation report showed seed valuations rose **15.4% YoY**, but early-stage AI in Europe still trails top U.S. pricing. [PitchBook Q1 2025 Europe](https://pitchbook.com/news/reports/q1-2025-european-vc-valuations-report)

## Key Conclusion

Yes, AI still commands a premium.

No, Provance should **not** currently price itself as a top-decile institutional AI seed company.

Why:

- no product is live yet
- no benchmark or peer-reviewed performance is published yet
- no design partners are documented yet
- no recurring revenue exists yet
- this is a trust-sensitive category where investors will demand proof, not just story

## Recommended Financing Strategy

## Stage 0: Optional Bridge / Founder Round

### Recommendation

Raise only if necessary:

- **$75k-$200k**
- founder network, angels, friends-and-family, grants, or non-dilutive sources
- instrument: simple SAFE or founder-friendly note

### Use

- initial setup
- dataset acquisition
- benchmark harness
- early prototype work

### Valuation Guidance

- **$2.5M-$4M SAFE cap**

### Why

This is not the main round. It is only useful if you need to create enough signal to run a proper pre-seed process.

## Stage 1: Recommended Institutional Pre-Seed

### My Recommendation

This is the round Provance should target first.

- **Raise target:** `$500k-$750k`
- **Stretch ceiling:** `up to $1M`
- **Instrument:** post-money SAFE
- **Target cap range:** `$5M-$7M`
- **Opening ask recommendation:** `$650k on a $6M post-money SAFE cap`

### Why This Is The Right Target

This round size is large enough to:

- build the benchmark and dataset program
- ship the first working image MVP
- create sample reports and trust assets
- secure early design partners
- avoid an underfunded half-build

But it is still small enough to:

- close without requiring full seed-grade traction
- avoid overpricing the company before proof exists
- preserve flexibility for the next round

### Why Not Raise More Right Now

Raising `>$1M` before product proof risks:

- valuation pushback
- a long fundraising cycle
- over-dilution through stacked SAFEs
- future round pressure if milestones slip

### Why Not Use The PDF's Higher Seed Framing As The Immediate Plan

The PDF's later-stage seed framing of `$1M-$2M` at `$6M-$10M pre-money` becomes more credible **after**:

- benchmarked performance
- a live product
- early pilots
- evidence of demand

It is not the strongest first raise posture from today's starting point.

## Stage 2: Seed Round

### When To Raise

Raise this only after Provance has all or most of:

- image MVP in production or controlled pilot
- benchmark report and methodology posture
- 3-5 design partners or pilot customers
- at least 2-3 repeat paying customers or equivalent validated contracts
- trust asset package: sample forensic report, methodology page, landing page, security summary

### My Recommendation

- **Raise target:** `$1.5M-$2.5M`
- **Stretch ceiling:** `up to $3M`
- **Target valuation:** `$10M-$14M pre-money`
- **Strong-case upside:** `up to $16M pre-money` only if traction quality is real

### Why This Range

This range is more defensible than the original PDF's `$6M-$10M pre-money` seed framing because:

- current seed market medians have moved up materially
- AI companies still receive a premium
- the company can credibly claim a differentiated category once product proof exists

At the same time, it is deliberately **below hype-tier AI pricing** because Provance is not a foundation-model company and should not pretend to be one.

## Dilution Guardrails

### Pre-Seed

Target dilution:

- `8% to 14%`

Example:

- `$650k` on `$6M` post-money SAFE cap = about `10.8%`

### Seed

Target dilution:

- `15% to 20%`

### Total Dilution Target Before Series A

Try to keep:

- founder dilution from cash rounds + option pool in a range that still leaves strong control and future flexibility
- founder ownership above `65%` after pre-seed + seed if possible for a solo-founder company

## Use Of Funds Recommendation

### Pre-Seed Allocation

- `35%` product and full-stack engineering
- `25%` ML, data, and benchmark development
- `15%` design and landing page / trust assets
- `10%` cloud, tooling, and security baseline
- `10%` pilot acquisition and customer discovery
- `5%` legal and formation

### Seed Allocation

- `40%` team expansion
- `25%` product and ML scale
- `15%` GTM and pilot-to-paid conversion
- `10%` legal / compliance / trust validation
- `10%` infrastructure and operations

## Valuation Methods To Defend The Number

Provance should defend valuation using a blended method:

### 1. Stage Benchmark Method

Use current market medians as the anchor, not as the final answer.

### 2. Scorecard Method

Adjust for:

- category urgency
- technical complexity
- founder quality
- defensibility
- proof level
- investor appetite for AI / trust infrastructure

### 3. Milestone-Based Narrative

Tie valuation to what the round unlocks:

- benchmark credibility
- first production product
- first pilots
- trust asset package

### 4. Comparable Positioning Method

Do not compare Provance to frontier labs.

Do compare it to:

- applied AI infrastructure
- verification / trust-tech
- enterprise risk and fraud tooling
- forensic workflow software

## Investor Fit

Best investor profiles:

- pre-seed and seed funds that back applied AI
- trust and safety / cybersecurity / fraud / digital identity investors
- media-tech or legal-tech investors
- high-conviction angels comfortable with deep technical categories

Avoid:

- investors who only fund consumer growth stories
- investors expecting a broad AI assistant narrative
- generalists who cannot understand why forensic evidence matters

## Fundraising Narrative

The strongest story is:

Provance is not selling a detector. It is building trust infrastructure for synthetic media verification, starting with a wedge where evidence quality matters more than generic accuracy scores.

## Final Recommendation

### Round To Aim For Now

- **Primary recommendation:** `$650k` on a `$6M post-money SAFE cap`

### Acceptable Range

- **Floor:** `$500k` on `$5M cap`
- **Stretch:** `$750k-$1M` on `$6.5M-$7M cap` if investor demand is strong

### Do Not Target Yet

- a full institutional seed narrative at top-decile AI pricing
- a round size that forces you to justify a company you have not yet built

## Bottom Line

Provance should raise enough to reach undeniable proof, not enough to simulate scale before validation.
