# IP Assignment Agreement Template Brief

## Important Note

This is a drafting brief and template outline, not legal advice.

Final agreement language must be reviewed by counsel.

## Purpose

Ensure all IP created for Provance is owned by the company, not by the founder personally or by contractors individually.

## Why This Matters

Investors will ask:

- who owns the code?
- who owns the models?
- who owns the datasets?
- who owns the brand assets?

If ownership is unclear, diligence becomes painful fast.

## Minimum Agreement Sections

### 1. Parties

- assigning individual or contractor
- company receiving the rights

### 2. Definitions

- intellectual property
- work product
- confidential information
- inventions
- software
- datasets
- documentation

### 3. Assignment Clause

The assignor irrevocably assigns to the company all rights, title, and interest in the defined work product created within the scope of work.

### 4. Future Assignment Language

Include present-tense assignment language where permitted so future-created work is also clearly covered.

### 5. Moral Rights Waiver

Include to the extent permitted by law.

### 6. Further Assurances

The assignor agrees to sign future documents needed to perfect ownership.

### 7. Confidentiality

Any non-public company information remains confidential.

### 8. Excluded Prior IP

List any pre-existing materials the assignor is not transferring.

### 9. Governing Law

Defined by jurisdiction and counsel.

## Who Must Sign

- founder
- every contractor
- every employee
- any technical advisor contributing material work

## Assets That Must Be Covered

- source code
- model weights
- prompts and internal systems
- training pipelines
- benchmark code
- datasets and labels where assignable
- product designs
- logos and brand assets
- reports and documentation

## Practical Rule

No one should contribute meaningful work to Provance without signed IP assignment and confidentiality coverage.
