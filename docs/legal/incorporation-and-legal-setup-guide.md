# Incorporation And Legal Setup Guide

## Important Note

This is an operating guide, not legal advice.

All final legal documents should be reviewed by qualified counsel in the chosen jurisdiction.

## Objective

Define the minimum legal setup Provance should complete before:

- taking institutional money
- issuing equity formally
- hiring
- entering commercial contracts

## Recommended Structure

If the goal is global venture fundraising, the default investor-friendly structure is usually:

- Delaware C-Corp at the parent level

If operations require local market execution, local subsidiaries or operating entities can be layered later.

This recommendation is based on fundraising practicality, not nationalism or tax advice.

## Minimum Documents At Formation

- certificate of incorporation
- bylaws
- founder board or incorporator resolutions
- founder stock purchase / issuance documents
- stock ledger / cap table
- EIN / tax registration equivalents

## Founder Setup Requirements

- founder stock issuance recorded properly
- vesting decision made explicitly
- IP assignment signed
- confidentiality obligations documented

## Legal Documents Required Before Raising

- incorporation records
- IP assignment agreement
- SAFE template or preferred financing instrument
- cap table
- founder approvals / resolutions

## Legal Documents Required Before Public Launch

- terms of service
- privacy policy
- acceptable use policy
- security statement summary
- retention / deletion policy

## Legal Documents Required Before Hiring

- contractor agreement
- employment agreement
- invention assignment / confidentiality language
- option plan later when equity compensation begins

## Recommended Immediate Sequence

1. form company
2. issue founder stock properly
3. sign IP assignment
4. create cap table
5. prepare SAFE
6. prepare basic launch policies later

## Decision Questions To Resolve With Counsel

- jurisdiction of incorporation
- founder vesting structure
- number of authorized shares
- board structure
- tax consequences
- data protection obligations by customer geography

## What Not To Delay

- founder stock issuance
- IP ownership cleanup
- cap table hygiene

These become painful and expensive if done late.
