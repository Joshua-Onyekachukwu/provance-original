# Investment Memo

## Company

Provance

## Working Product

VerifAI

## One-Line Thesis

Provance is building explainable trust infrastructure for synthetic media verification, starting with a forensic platform that helps journalists, investigators, legal teams, and enterprises determine whether media is real, AI-generated, or uncertain.

## The Problem

Synthetic media quality has improved faster than verification infrastructure. Most available tools return opaque scores that are difficult to operationalize in legal, editorial, fraud, and enterprise workflows.

High-stakes users need:

- evidence, not just detection
- explainability, not just confidence scores
- attribution, not just binary flags
- workflow outputs, not just raw model responses

## The Solution

Provance combines:

- multi-signal media forensics
- attribution intelligence
- explainable output
- report generation
- workflow-ready APIs

The product is designed as a trust system, not just a classifier.

## Why Now

- AI is capturing a growing share of venture and enterprise attention, with funding concentrated around AI-native categories. [Reuters / PitchBook summary](https://www.reuters.com/business/us-ai-startups-see-funding-surge-while-more-vc-funds-struggle-raise-data-shows-2025-07-15/)
- AngelList reported that **41.5%** of deals in H1 2025 went to AI/ML startups, confirming continued capital concentration in AI. [AngelList H1 2025](https://www.angellist.com/data-center/the-state-of-venture-1h25)
- Regulatory, enterprise, and reputational pressure around synthetic media is increasing.
- Existing detection tools leave room for a product focused on evidence-grade workflows and legal/operational trust.

## Market Opportunity

Provance sits at the intersection of:

- AI verification
- digital forensics
- fraud prevention
- trust and safety
- legal workflow software

The immediate TAM should not be framed as "all AI." It should be framed as the set of workflows where media authenticity materially changes decisions.

## Why This Wins

Provance should win on:

- explainable evidence
- forensic report workflows
- model attribution
- chain-of-custody-aware outputs
- a growing fingerprint dataset and retraining moat

This differs from:

- API-first moderation products optimized for scale
- provenance-first products focused on point-of-capture integrity
- generic enterprise deepfake detection platforms optimized for channel security

## Defensibility

The moat is not the first model release.

The moat is:

- proprietary labeled dataset accumulation
- attribution fingerprint graph
- explainability and reporting workflow
- trust credibility through methodology and benchmark discipline

## Risks

- dataset quality and model drift
- false positives in high-trust workflows
- long enterprise and legal sales cycles
- legal claims outrunning product maturity
- compute cost expansion if video is introduced too early

## Milestones For The Next Round

The next institutional raise should be supported by:

- a benchmarked MVP
- a working image verification product
- sample forensic report and methodology assets
- early pilot customers
- first recurring revenue or equivalent design-partner proof
